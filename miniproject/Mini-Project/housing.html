Frequently Asked Questions

Who is responsible for maintaining the housing in the society?

The responsibility for maintaining the housing in a society typically lies with the property owner or landlord. In the case of a society, this may be the society maintenance team or a third-party management company.

What should I do if I encounter a problem with my housing unit?

If you encounter a problem with your housing unit, such as a leaky roof or broken appliance, you should immediately contact the society maintenance team through the e society management portal.

How can I report a housing code violation in my society?

If you believe that your housing unit or the society as a whole is in violation of local housing codes, you should contact the local housing authority or code enforcement office. They will be able to inspect the property and take the necessary steps to bring it into compliance with the housing codes.

How can I resolve a dispute with my landlord or neighbors in the society?

If you have a dispute with your landlord or neighbors in the society, you may be able to resolve it through mediation or arbitration. In some cases, you may also be able to take legal action. It is recommended that you seek the advice of a legal professional or a local community organization for assistance.
